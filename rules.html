1st day

            Govatsa Dwadashi is considered as the first day of Deepavali. 
            This year, Govatsa Dwadashi is on Thursday, November 12, 2020. 
            Pradoshkala Govatsa Dwadashi muhurat is from 05:29 pm to 08:07 pm (Duration: 02 hours 39 mins) 
            
            Dwadashi tithi begins at 12:40 am on Nov 12, 2020 
            Dwadashi Tithi ends at 09:30 m on Nov 12, 2020 
            
            Govatsa Dwadashi is celebrated one day before Dhanteras. On this day cows and calves are worshipped. 
            In Maharashtra Govatsa Dwadashi is known as Vasu Baras.

2nd day

            
Dhanteras

            This year Dhanteras Puja will be observed on Friday, November 13, 2020. 
            Dhanteras Puja muhurat is from 05:28 pm to 05:59 pm. (Duration: 30 mins)

            Pradosh Kaal is from 05:28 pm to 08:07 pm
            Vrishabha Kaal is from 05:32 pm to 07:28 pm
            
Dhanvantari Trayodashi puja

            Dhanvantari Trayodashi is observed two days before Diwali Puja.
            The day is celebrated as the birth anniversary of Lord Dhanvantari, the teacher and the father of Ayurveda.

            This year Dhanvantari Puja is on Friday, November 13, 2020.
            Dhanvantari puja pratahkala muhurat is from 06:42 am to 08:51 am (Duration: 02 hours 09 mins)

            Trayodashi tithi begins at 09:30 PM on Nov 12, 2020
            Trayodashi tithi ends at 05:59 PM on Nov 13, 2020
            
Yama Deepam

             A Deepak is lit outside the home on Trayodashi tithi. 
            It is believed that Deepak pleases Lord Yama and he protects family members from any accidental death.

            You can lit Yama Deepam on Friday, November 13, 2020. Yama Deepam sayan sandhya is from 05:28 pm to 05:59 pm (Duration: 30 mins)

            
Kali Chaudas

            Kali Chaudas is observed during Chaturdashi Tithi during Diwali festivity. 
            This year Kali Chaudas is on Friday, November 13, 2020. 
            Kali Chaudas muhurat is from 11:39 pm on November 13 to 12:32 am on November 14. (Duration: 53 mins)

            Chaturdashi tithi begins at 05:59 pm on Nov 13, 2020
            Chaturdashi tithi ends at 02:17 pm on Nov 14, 2020
            
            
Hanuman Puja

            Hanuman Puja is performed a day before Diwali Puja in some parts of India especially in Gujarat. 
            The day of Hanuman Puja coincides with Kali Chaudas. Hence, Deepavali Hanuman Puja will be observed on Friday, November 13, 2020. 
            The puja muhurat is from 11:39 pm to 12:32 am, Nov 14 (Duration: 53 mins)

3rd day

        
Lakshmi Puja

        Lakshmi Puja is done during Pradosh Kaal which starts after sunset and approximately lasts for 2 hours and 24 minutes. 
        This year, Lakshmi Puja is on Saturday, November 14, 2020. Lakshmi puja muhurat is from 05:28 pm to 07:24 pm (Duration: 01 hour 56 mins)

        Pradosh Kaal: 05:28 pm to 08:07 pm
        Vrishabha Kaal: 05:28 pm to 07:24 pm
        Amavasya tithi begins at 02:17 pm on Nov 14, 2020 and ends at 10:36 am on Nov 15, 2020

        
Chopda Puja/ Sharda Puja

         In Gujarat Lakshmi Puja is known as Chopda Puja or Sharda Puja. Auspicious Choghadiya muhurat for Diwali Chopda puja (November 14)

        Afternoon Muhurat (Chara, Labha, Amrita): 02:17 pm to 04:07 pm
        Evening Muhurat (Labha): 05:28 pm to 07:07 pm
        Night Muhurat (Shubha, Amrita, Chara): 08:47 pm to 01:45 am, Nov 15
        Early Morning Muhurat (Labha): 05:04 am to 06:44 am, Nov 15

        
Kali Puja

         Kali Puja is a Hindu festival celebrated on new moon day during Diwali festivity. 
         This year Kali Puja is on Saturday, November 14, 2020. Kali Puja Nishita time is from 11:39 pm to 12:32 am on Nov 15. (Duration: 53 mins)

4th day

        
Govardhan Puja
 
        Govardhan Puja is the day when Lord Krishna defeated God Indra. It usually falls the next day after Diwali Puja. 
        This year Govardhan Puja will be observed on Sunday, November 15, 2020. 
        Govardhan Puja Sayankala muhurat is from 03:19 pm to 05:27 pm. (Duration: 02 hours 09 mins)

        Gowardhan Puja is also known as Annakut Puja. The same day in Maharashtra is celebrated as Bali Pratipada or Bali Padva.

5th day

        
Bhaiya Dooj
 
        On Bhaiya Dooj, sisters perform Tika ceremony and pray for their brothers long and happy lives. 
        This year Bhai Dooj is on Monday, November 16, 2020.

        Dwitiya tithi begins at 07:06 am on Nov 16, 2020
        Dwitiya tithi ends at 03:56 am on Nov 17, 2020
        Bhaiya Dooj is also known as Bhau Beej, Bhatra Dwitiya, Bhai Dwitiya and Bhathru Dwithiya.
